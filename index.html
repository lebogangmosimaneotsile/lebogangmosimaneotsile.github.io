About

A little about me

My journey into UX wasn't linear; it started with understanding people. I hold a BA in Psychology and Sociology from the University of Botswana, where I studied human behaviour, social dynamics, and the factors that shape how people think, interact, and make decisions.

After graduating, I developed an interest in technology and began teaching myself front-end development, eventually earning a certificate in Front-End Web Development through Udemy. Learning how to build digital products introduced me to the technical side of creating experiences — but it also made me question how those experiences were designed in the first place.

That curiosity led me to UX design: a field that sits at the intersection of psychology, sociology, and technology. I became deeply interested in understanding not just how products function, but how they make people feel, how accessible they are, and whether they truly solve real human problems.

Today, I combine research-driven thinking, design strategy, and technical knowledge to create user-centered digital experiences that are both functional and meaningful.

Banking Without Borders — Mobile-First Bank Account Setup

Designed a mobile-first onboarding experience for two underserved user groups in South Africa — first-time account holders and foreign nationals. The redesign addressed both logistical barriers (no branch access, document confusion) and psychological ones (fear of rejection as a foreign national). Two rounds of usability testing validated the approach.

"Everything makes sense. Looks very easy to use. Better than ABSA dare I say."— Usability test participant
